About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Information Technology at Rutgers University (MSIS Department), holding a Master's degree in Statistics from Columbia University. My research focuses on advancing the causal inference capabilities of large language models (LLMs), with an emphasis on building interpretable, transparent, and reliable systems that move not only on pattern matching but intervention-based and counterfactual reasoning.

Integrating my backgrounds in computer science, statistics, and business analytics, I apply AI and causal methods to examine complex business phenomena,such as managerial decision-making, stock price dynamics, and customer behavior modeling. My goal is to develop technically rigorous yet practically impactful AI systems that support data-driven decision-making in modern enterprises.
